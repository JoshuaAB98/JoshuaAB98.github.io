Take a Reusable Bag

Taking your own reusable bags can greatly help the environment as the average person uses 13,000 plastic bags over a lifetime which each can take up to 100 years to degrade and often end up in oceans killing many birds and sea animals. There is also a monetary benefit with each bag costing at least 5p. In total that adds up to a potential minimum of £650 on plastic bags over your lifetime.

Be Packaging Mindful

Packaging that items come in is sometimes unnecessary and often useless. One example of wasteful packaging is toothpaste tubes, which are already their own container. However, most toothpaste tubes also come in card boxes which are a waste of card/paper. Also, bigger versions of items such as 2 litre bottles of cola will use less packaging per litre than a 500ml bottle does.

Buy Used

Buying second hand doesn’t get eco-friendlier. Buying something that’s already been used instead of buying new is 100% eco-friendly and often some items such as furniture may have some interesting story behind them. Ikea are doing amazingly with their current 70% renewable/recycled material usage with their goal of 100% renewability by 2030, but using no new materials is still better than using 100% renewable materials.
